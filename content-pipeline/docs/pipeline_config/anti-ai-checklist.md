## SECTION 3 RULES BLOCK
*(Load this section only for Stages 3 and 4)*

### Prohibited — zero tolerance

- No em dashes anywhere — use commas, colons, semicolons, or parentheses instead
- No filler affirmations: "Certainly," "Great question," "Absolutely," "It's worth noting," "Delve into," "Let's explore," "Without further ado," or any variant
- No impersonal hedging: "It is important to note that," "It should be mentioned that," "One might consider"
- No generic transitions as substitutes for logical connection: "In addition," "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Moving on," "As we have seen"

### Structural anti-patterns — must be absent

- No three consecutive paragraphs of the same length
- No perfectly parallel list structures where all items follow the exact same grammatical form and length
- No H3s that exist only to sub-categorize with no argumentative or instructive function
- No symmetrical 3-sentence paragraph chains

### Voice authenticity requirements — must be present

- At least two single-sentence paragraphs in the body
- At least one paragraph over 5 sentences in the body
- At least one direct stated opinion or assertion per major H2
- Active voice throughout; passive only where natural and intentional
- At least one moment where the writer's specific perspective is identifiable

## FULL CHECKLIST
*(Load for Stage 6 QA and standalone audits)*

### SECTION 1: PEOPLE-FIRST CONTENT STANDARDS

The post exists to help a person, not to rank for a keyword.
- [ ] Answers a real question a real person would type into a search engine
- [ ] Provides enough information for the reader to make a meaningful decision without contacting the client
- [ ] Does not withhold genuinely useful information to push conversion
- [ ] H1 accurately describes the value delivered; not misleading or shock-driven
- [ ] Adds original value; not primarily a summary of existing sources

The post leaves the reader better equipped than when they arrived.
- [ ] Reader finishes knowing something specific they did not know before
- [ ] At least one section more specific or useful than top-ranking competitor posts
- [ ] Does not leave a major obvious reader question unanswered
- [ ] Reader would feel satisfied without needing to search again

The post is not commodity content.
- [ ] Contains at least one non-commodity angle: firsthand experience, proprietary insight, expert judgment, or original analysis
- [ ] Could not be substantially replicated by a generative AI model without client-specific input
- [ ] No section exists solely to capture keyword variants rather than serve the reader

### SECTION 2: E-E-A-T COMPLIANCE

**EXPERIENCE**
- [ ] Named real-world case, practitioner observation, verifiable local detail, or source-cited statistic present
- [ ] No section relies entirely on hypothetical scenarios as its only evidence
- [ ] At least one element could only come from direct exposure to this industry or topic

**EXPERTISE**
- [ ] Mechanism-level understanding demonstrated: not just what, but why and how
- [ ] Technical claims accurate and appropriately termed for the audience
- [ ] At least one direct opinion or assertion per major section
- [ ] At least one counterintuitive or non-obvious insight present

**AUTHORITATIVENESS**
- [ ] Post establishes why this client has standing on this topic
- [ ] External sources are authoritative: government, peer-reviewed, industry standards, or major media with named data
- [ ] No unsourced superlatives without supporting evidence

**TRUSTWORTHINESS**
- [ ] Every empirical claim cites a source inline
- [ ] Sources accessible, current within 3 years unless foundational, support the specific claim
- [ ] YMYL topics include credentialed source or explicit acknowledgment of limits
- [ ] Post makes no guarantees it cannot support

### SECTION 3: ANTI-AI WRITING STANDARDS

Prohibited elements — zero tolerance:
- [ ] No em dashes anywhere
- [ ] No filler affirmations: "Certainly," "Great question," "Absolutely," "It's worth noting," "Delve into," "Let's explore," "Without further ado," or any variant
- [ ] No impersonal hedging: "It is important to note that," "It should be mentioned that," "One might consider"
- [ ] No generic transitions as substitutes for logical connection: "In addition," "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Moving on," "As we have seen"

Structural anti-patterns — must be absent:
- [ ] No three consecutive paragraphs of the same length
- [ ] No perfectly parallel list structures where all items follow the exact same grammatical form and length
- [ ] No H3s that exist only to sub-categorize with no argumentative or instructive function
- [ ] No symmetrical 3-sentence paragraph chains

Voice authenticity requirements — must be present:
- [ ] At least two single-sentence paragraphs in the body
- [ ] At least one paragraph over 5 sentences in the body
- [ ] At least one direct stated opinion or assertion per major H2
- [ ] Active voice throughout; passive only where natural and intentional
- [ ] At least one moment where the writer's specific perspective is identifiable

### SECTION 4: FORMATTING AND TECHNICAL STANDARDS

Headers and structure:
- [ ] One H1 only
- [ ] H2s address distinct reader problems, not topical categories
- [ ] H3s support the argument of their parent H2
- [ ] No bold text used in place of H3s
- [ ] Lists used intentionally; no list has fewer than 2 or more than 6 items

SEO elements:
- [ ] Meta title 50–60 characters
- [ ] Meta description 140–160 characters, benefit-first, includes primary keyword
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1, first 100 words, at least one H2, meta description
- [ ] Primary keyword density does not exceed 1.5%
- [ ] 2–3 internal links with natural anchor text

CTA:
- [ ] CTA specifies an action
- [ ] CTA tone matches reader intent stage and post register
- [ ] CTA does not contradict the helpfulness standard
