## 2. AUDIENCE DEFINITION

The primary reader is a home service business owner — a plumber, HVAC technician, landscaper, or cleaning company operator — running a 3–20 person operation in a competitive local market. They built their business on referrals and word-of-mouth, but growth has plateaued. They have tried Google Ads or lead-gen platforms like HomeAdvisor and have experienced inconsistent returns, rising costs, or leads that don't convert. They understand they need a stronger online presence but feel intimidated by marketing jargon and skeptical of agencies that overpromise. They are not looking for a comprehensive digital marketing education — they want to know if content marketing is actually worth doing, and if so, what to do first. After reading this post, they should be able to describe what content marketing is in plain terms, understand why it works specifically for local service businesses, and identify two to three concrete first steps they can take this week.

## 6. STYLE DIRECTIVE

**Register:** Conversational-Practical. Semi-formal (3/5). Treat the reader as a smart operator running a real business, not as a student.

**Opening Scene:** Open in the frustration — a business owner who has been paying for ads for 18 months and still can't predict next month's call volume. Don't open with a definition of content marketing. Open with the problem.

**Vocabulary to Use:**
- "Steady flow of organic leads"
- "Appearing in the right searches"
- "Competitive market"
- "Drive real business results"
- "Position your business as..."
- "Stand out in a competitive market"
- "Top of mind"

**Vocabulary to Avoid:**
- "Game-changing"
- "Disrupt"
- "Bleeding edge"
- "Leverage" (unless natural in context)
- Any jargon left unexplained (E-E-A-T, domain authority, SERP must be avoided or immediately explained if used)

**Tone Notes:**
- Acknowledge skepticism early. This audience has heard marketing promises before. Earn their trust with data before making claims.
- Don't talk down. They know their trade deeply. They just don't know marketing deeply. Respect that.
- Practical over inspirational. Every section should leave the reader with something they can do or evaluate.
- No em dashes in final copy.

**Paragraph Rhythm:** Short to medium (2–4 sentences). Average sentence length 15–20 words. Never more than 6 sentences in a paragraph.
